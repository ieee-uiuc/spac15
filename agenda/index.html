ECS Workshop with Amy Fruehling

Career Services is a passion Amy brings to her role as director of Engineering Career Services. Prior to joining ECS in January 2014, Amy worked with hundreds of business students from Freshman to MBA as well as recruiters in the College of Business, over the past decade to ensure the successful transition of students into new professionals. Prior to joining Illinois, Amy’s career in marketing spanned several industries, including the advertising business, grocery and financial services industries.
4:15 PM

Professional Development Workshop, hosted by

Qualcomm

Making the transition from student to young adult entering the workforce can be tough as you encounter many new challenges like relocation, shift in accountability, adjusting to a new environment, realizing your new “social status”, learning outside of school, translating day 1, and understanding how to interpret, gather, and deal with feedback. We’ll review key areas you need to be aware of and accomplish during your first 90 days of employment to ensure success in your new role. Topics of discussion include: knowing the basics, understanding what’s expected of you, brand management, engagement, and owning your career. You won’t want to miss this!

Mark J. Laufenberg, Ph.D., President of

PowerWorld

Dr. Laufenberg has been the President of PowerWorld Corporation since co-founding the company in 1996. PowerWorld, based in Champaign, Illinois, develops and distributes software for the simulation and visualization of large-scale electric power grids. Their products have been sold to over 700 separate corporate entities in 70 different countries around the world. PowerWorld's customer list includes many utilities, both foreign and domestic, and many regulatory agencies, consulting firms, trading firms, and universities. Dr. Laufenberg received his Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering in 1997 from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign and has been the Principal Investigator on several National Science Foundation Small Business Innovation Research grants.

Dr. Laufenberg's speech is titled:
"Building a Startup Based on Technology Developed in My Time at UIUC"
6:30 PM

Bryan G. Lammers, Division Manager at

Caterpillar

Bryan currently leads a division within Caterpillar Electronics with responsibility for major systems' controls and mining equipment controls. This includes fly-by-wire steering, transmission, implements, braking, electric drive, and hybrid systems at the subsystem level, and the integration of all systems for all mining equipment at the machine level. He has more than 30 years of experience in Manufacturing, Marketing and Product Development - all at Caterpillar. Within manufacturing, he has worked in assembly, machining, fabrications, and heat treatment. In product development, he has worked in structures, power trains, analysis, hydraulics, and electronics. He holds patents in all of the above disciplines.

Mr. Lammers' speech is titled:
"So, You Think You Know What Your Career Will Be... You Have No Idea!"
